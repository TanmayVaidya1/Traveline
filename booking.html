Safari Booking

The oldest and largest national park of Maharashtra, which is named after a God worshipped by the local tribes, Tadoba National Park is a wildlife reserve located in the Chandrapur district of the state. The national park is a prominent part of Tadoba Andhari Tiger Reserve, which is the country’s one of 47 tiger reserve projects. Covering an area of 1,727 square kilometres including the national park, the wildlife reserve is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, including tigers, leopards, sloth bears, Indian bison, and a variety of bird species.

Tadoba Andhari Tiger Reserve, which is also named after the Andhari River that flows through the forest, is known for its dense forests, scenic landscapes, and rich biodiversity. It was established as a protected area in 1955 and was amalgamated with the national park as Andhari Wildlife Sanctuary in 1995. In recent years, Tadoba has become a popular destination for wildlife enthusiasts and photographers, offering visitors a chance to witness some of India's most iconic and endangered wildlife species in their natural habitat.
